# Summit Positioning Decision Memo (2026-03-11)

## Summit Readiness Assertion
Summit will lead with a **governance and GRC-first buyer motion** while preserving a developer-ready integration path for orchestrator ecosystems.

## Decision (Final)
For the next quarter, Summit should lean harder into **governance and GRC-first buyers** (Risk, Compliance, Security Architecture) and package developer-platform messaging as the execution path, not the headline.

## Why this is the highest-leverage posture now
1. **Category drift has moved to orchestration-first narratives.**
   OpenAI Symphony, UiPath Maestro, and Kore.ai are teaching buyers to treat orchestration as the default control plane. Competing as another generic orchestrator reduces differentiation.
2. **Enterprise urgency is governance-timed, not orchestration-timed.**
   EU AI Act obligations and governance-first RAG expectations create immediate budget pressure in risk and compliance organizations.
3. **Summit differentiation is strongest in graph-grounded provenance.**
   Summit can own retrieval-native controls, auditable lineage, and evidence-linked GraphRAG outputs where connector-heavy orchestration vendors are comparatively weak.
4. **This positioning still supports partnerships instead of platform wars.**
   Summit can sit under Symphony/UiPath/Kore/Perplexity-style control planes as the knowledge runtime and governance substrate.

## One-line positioning (approved)
> Summit is the **graph-native knowledge and governance runtime** that plugs under orchestrators to deliver provenance-rich, policy-aligned, and auditable agent outcomes.

- **Primary promise:** "Deploy governed GraphRAG with audit-ready provenance without replacing your orchestrator."
- **Secondary audience:** Platform engineering teams implementing orchestration and memory layers.
