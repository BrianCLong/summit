---

## Quick Reference

### When We Win
✅ Customer frustrated with Palantir deployment timelines (6-12 months)
✅ Budget-conscious agencies (not top-tier IC with unlimited budgets)
✅ New CIO/CDO mandate for modernization
✅ Recompete of expiring Palantir contract
✅ Air-gapped or edge deployment requirements
✅ Customer values vendor diversity (anti-lock-in)

❌ Relationship trumps technology (10+ year incumbent)

- Deep relationships at senior levels (Director-level, SES)
- Embedded in workflows at CIA, NSA, FBI, SOCOM

**How to Counter**:

> for a faster, more cost-effective alternative that delivers equivalent capability,
> that's where we come in.
>
> Let's run a pilot and let the technology speak for itself."

## Weaknesses (Palantir)

### 1. Slow Deployment Timelines
- **6-12 months** typical for enterprise deployment
- Requires extensive professional services (Palantir + SIs)
- Custom ontology development (months of iteration)

**How to Exploit**:
> "Palantir is powerful, but it takes 6-12 months to deploy. For agencies facing
> urgent threats or tight deadlines, that's a non-starter.
>
> We've designed Summit for speed: **30-day golden path deployment**. You can have
> analysts using the platform for real cases within a month, not a year.
>
> **Example**: DIA's pilot went from kickoff to production in 28 days. Palantir's
> competing project took 9 months.
>
> If time matters, we have a significant advantage."

**Proof Points**:
- DIA case study (28-day deployment)
- CYBERCOM JWICS deployment (45 days vs. 12 months typical)
- Customer testimonials on speed

### 2. High Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)
- **$5M-$10M+** annual license fees for enterprise (50-100 analysts)
- **$10M-$50M+** in professional services (ontology, integration, training)
- **5-year TCO**: Often $25M-$50M+

**How to Exploit**:
> "Palantir's pricing is opaque and often shocks customers once all costs are in:
> license fees, professional services, ongoing maintenance.
>
> Our pricing is transparent:
>   • $500K-$1M/year for 50 analysts (80% less than Palantir)
>   • Minimal services required (golden path = self-service)
>   • 5-year TCO: $3M-$5M vs. Palantir's $25M-$50M
>
> That's a **40-80% cost savings** for equivalent capability.
>
> Let me show you the TCO model—I'll use conservative assumptions, and you tell me
> if the math works."

**Proof Points**:
- TCO comparison spreadsheet (Excel model)
- Customer testimonials on cost savings
- Public Palantir contract values (SAM.gov, FOIA)

### 3. Vendor Lock-In

- **No export portability**: Data trapped in Palantir format

> "One concern we hear about Palantir is lock-in. Once you're in the ecosystem—
> ontology, workflows, analyst training—it's very hard to leave.
>
> We've designed Summit to be open:
>   • **Standard APIs** (GraphQL, REST) for integration
>   • **Export options** (CSV, JSON, Gephi, Neo4j native)
>   • **Interoperable** with other tools (Palantir, Splunk, Elastic)
>
> You're not locked in. If a better tool comes along in 3 years, you can migrate
> without starting over.
>
> If vendor diversity and long-term flexibility matter, that's a differentiator."

**Proof Points**:
- API documentation (public or sample)
- Integration architecture diagram
- Customer case of Summit → Palantir coexistence

## Common Objections & Responses

### Objection 1: "Palantir is the standard. Why should we take a risk on Summit?"

**Bad Response**:
❌ "We're just as good as Palantir!"
❌ "Palantir is overpriced and slow!"

**Good Response**:
✅ **Acknowledge + Reframe + Proof**

```
"Palantir is absolutely the standard—they've earned that over 20 years. The question
isn't whether Palantir is good; it's whether it's the best fit for YOUR specific
needs today.

What we hear from agencies is:
  • 6-12 month deployments don't match the urgency of modern threats
  • $10M+ budgets are hard to justify in constrained environments
  • Vendor lock-in limits flexibility as technology evolves

If those concerns resonate, we have a compelling alternative: **same mission outcomes,
80% faster deployment, 40-80% lower cost**.

We don't ask you to take our word for it. Let's run a **60-day pilot** with real
data and real analysts. If we don't deliver, no harm done. If we do, you'll have
the data to make an informed decision.

Fair?"
```

**Proof Points**:
- Pilot proposal (low-risk, success-based)
- DIA case study (side-by-side comparison)
- TCO model (quantified savings)

### Objection 2: "We've already invested $X million in Palantir. We can't just switch."

**Bad Response**:
❌ "Sunk cost fallacy—don't throw good money after bad!"
❌ "Palantir will keep bleeding you dry!"

**Good Response**:
✅ **Empathize + Complement + Expand**

```
"I completely understand. You've invested heavily in Palantir—ontology, training,
workflows. Switching entirely would be disruptive and expensive.

Here's what we're seeing work at other agencies:
  • **Complement, don't replace**: Use Summit for new use cases or divisions where
    Palantir deployment timelines are a bottleneck.
  • **Pilot for expansion**: If you're adding 100 analysts next year, pilot Summit
    for that cohort instead of expanding Palantir licenses.
  • **Interoperability**: Summit integrates with Palantir via APIs. Analysts can
    use both tools where it makes sense.

**Example**: One agency kept Palantir for their core Counter-Terrorism team (10-year
investment, highly customized) but deployed Summit for Cyber and Fraud divisions
(new use cases, needed speed). Best of both worlds.

If you're not looking to rip-and-replace, but want optionality for growth or new
missions, we can coexist with Palantir."
```

**Proof Points**:
- Coexistence architecture diagram
- Customer case of Summit + Palantir hybrid
- Integration API documentation

---

### Objection 3: "Palantir has FedRAMP High. You don't."

**Bad Response**:
❌ "FedRAMP doesn't matter for on-prem!"
❌ "We'll have it soon, just wait!"

**Good Response**:
✅ **Acknowledge + Alternative + Timeline**

```

Here's what we can do in the interim:
  1. **On-Prem Deployment**: If you prefer on-prem or air-gapped (which many agencies
     do for sensitive data), FedRAMP isn't required. We deploy on your infrastructure
     and inherit your ATO.
  2. **Hybrid Approach**: Start on-prem now, migrate to FedRAMP cloud later (when
     our cert is complete). No data migration required—same platform.
  3. **Accelerated ATO**: We'll provide our security package (SOC2, penetration tests,
     NIST 800-53 mapping) to fast-track your agency-specific ATO.

**Timeline Question**: When do you need to deploy? If it's within 6 months, on-prem
is faster anyway (no FedRAMP approval wait). If it's 12+ months, our FedRAMP cert
will be done.

What's your deployment timeline?"
```

**Proof Points**:
- SOC2 report (demonstrate security maturity)
- FedRAMP timeline and progress
- On-prem reference architecture

## Competitive Sales Strategy

### Stage 1: Discovery - Uncover Palantir Pain

**Questions to Ask**:

1. **"How long have you been using Palantir? What's working well, and where are the frustrations?"**
   - *Listen for*: Deployment delays, cost complaints, UX issues, lock-in concerns

2. **"When you deployed Palantir, how long did it take from contract to production?"**
   - *Listen for*: 6-12 months (use this to contrast with our 30 days)

3. **"What's your annual spend on Palantir—licenses plus services?"**
   - *Listen for*: $5M+ (use this to contrast with our $500K-$1M)

4. **"Are you planning to expand Palantir to new divisions or use cases? What's the timeline?"**
   - *Listen for*: Expansion plans (opportunity to offer Summit as alternative)

5. **"If you could change one thing about Palantir, what would it be?"**
   - *Listen for*: Specific pain points to address in demo
