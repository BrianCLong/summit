## 🎓 Sales Process by Stage

### Stage 1: Prospecting (Month 0-1)
**Goal**: Identify and qualify 10-15 target accounts per rep per quarter

**Key Activities**:
- [ ] Select accounts from [Top 100 list](./ICP-Strategy-2025.md#top-100-named-accounts)
- [ ] Research using [signals/triggers](./ICP-Strategy-2025.md#triggers--signals-high-fit-indicators)
- [ ] Launch [outbound campaign](./campaigns/Outbound-Campaign-Templates.md)
- [ ] Qualify using [BANT+ criteria](./playbooks/Gov-Defense-Intel-Playbook.md#qualification-criteria-bant)

**Success Metric**: 20% conversion from lead → qualified opportunity

---

### Stage 2: Discovery (Month 1-3)
**Goal**: Understand customer pain, quantify value, earn technical evaluation

**Key Activities**:
- [ ] Conduct [discovery call](./playbooks/Gov-Defense-Intel-Playbook.md#discovery-call-script-45-60-minutes)
- [ ] Build [ROI model](./tools/ROI-Calculator-Guide.md) with customer data
- [ ] Map buying committee (decision makers, technical evaluators, budget holders)
- [ ] Identify competitive landscape (Palantir? Custom build? Status quo?)

**Success Metric**: 50% conversion from qualified opp → technical evaluation

---

### Stage 3: Technical Evaluation (Month 3-9)
**Goal**: Prove technical fit and superior value via demo/pilot

**Key Activities**:
- [ ] Deliver [differentiated demo](./playbooks/Gov-Defense-Intel-Playbook.md#demo-strategy)
- [ ] Address [Palantir objections](./battlecards/Palantir-Gotham-Battlecard.md#common-objections--responses)
- [ ] Execute [60-90 day pilot](./playbooks/Gov-Defense-Intel-Playbook.md#pilotpoc-framework)
- [ ] Collect metrics, testimonials, and executive sponsorship

**Success Metric**: 60% conversion from pilot → procurement

---

### Stage 4: Procurement (Month 9-18)
**Goal**: Navigate RFP, negotiate contract, close deal

**Key Activities**:
- [ ] Support [RFP response](./playbooks/Gov-Defense-Intel-Playbook.md#rfp-response-strategy)
- [ ] Provide [pricing proposal](./playbooks/Gov-Defense-Intel-Playbook.md#pricing-strategy)
- [ ] Negotiate using [objection scripts](./battlecards/Palantir-Gotham-Battlecard.md#common-objections--responses)
- [ ] Close with [risk reversal](./playbooks/Gov-Defense-Intel-Playbook.md#closing-tactics)

**Success Metric**: 20-30% overall win rate (opp → closed-won)

---

### Stage 5: Onboarding & Expansion (Month 18-24)
**Goal**: Ensure customer success, identify upsell/cross-sell opportunities

**Key Activities**:
- [ ] Execute [customer handoff](./playbooks/Gov-Defense-Intel-Playbook.md#customer-success-handoff)
- [ ] Track success metrics (NPS, usage, outcomes)
- [ ] Identify [expansion triggers](./playbooks/Gov-Defense-Intel-Playbook.md#upsell-triggers)
- [ ] Generate case study and reference for new deals

**Success Metric**: 120% net revenue retention (upsell/expansion)

## 🎤 Common Scenarios & Quick Tips

### Scenario 1: Cold Call to Government CIO
**Goal**: Book 15-minute discovery call

**Script** (30 seconds):
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Summit. We work with agencies like DIA and
CYBERCOM to accelerate intelligence analysis—same outcomes as Palantir, but 30-day
deployment instead of 6-12 months, at 40-80% lower cost.

If you're exploring modernization or have a Palantir recompete coming up, I'd love
to share what we're seeing work. Worth a quick 15-minute call?"
```

**Objection**: "We're all set with Palantir."
**Response**: "Totally understand—Palantir is solid. Out of curiosity, when's your
contract up for renewal? If you're exploring alternatives, I'd love to share how
[Similar Agency] made the transition. Otherwise, I'll check back in [X months]."

---

### Scenario 2: Discovery Call - Palantir Incumbent
**Goal**: Identify pain points, build case for pilot

**Key Questions**:
1. "How long did Palantir take to deploy? What would faster time-to-value be worth?"
2. "What's your annual spend on Palantir—licenses plus services?"
3. "If budget wasn't a constraint, would you expand Palantir to more analysts? What's stopping you?"

**Next Step**: "Based on what you've shared, I think a 60-day pilot makes sense. We'll
use the same use case, same data, and compare results. If we don't beat Palantir on
speed, cost, and analyst satisfaction, you lose nothing. Fair?"

---

### Scenario 3: Proposal Stage - Price Objection
**Goal**: Justify investment with ROI, close deal

**Script**:
```
"I understand the budget concern. Let's look at the ROI together.

[Pull up ROI calculator]

Based on your 100 investigators and 20-hour average case time:
  • Summit reduces case time by 70% (to 6 hours)
  • That's 560,000 hours saved per year, or 269 FTE equivalents
  • At $100K per investigator, that's $26.9M in annual value

Summit costs $2M per year.

So the question isn't 'Is $2M too expensive?'—it's 'Is a 1,245% ROI worth it?'

What part of this math doesn't work for you?"
```

---

### Scenario 4: Post-Pilot - Converting to Contract
**Goal**: Use pilot success to close enterprise deal

**Script**:
```
"Based on the pilot results, here's what we've proven:
  ✓ 60% faster case resolution (your analysts' data, not ours)
  ✓ 92 NPS from analysts (vs. 57 for your legacy tools)
  ✓ Zero compliance findings during audit
  ✓ $5M projected annual savings (conservative ROI model)

Your analysts are asking when they can use Summit full-time. Your compliance team
wants to roll it out enterprise-wide.

What's preventing us from moving forward?"

[Address objections]

"Let's get the contract signed and start enterprise deployment next month. Your
team is ready—let's not keep them waiting."
```

## 📞 Escalation & Support

### When to Escalate

**To Sales Manager**:
- Deal stuck for >30 days with no clear path forward
- Customer requests discount >20%
- Competitive situation requires leadership involvement

**To Sales Engineering**:
- Complex technical questions (architecture, integrations)
- Custom demo required (>5 day lead time)
- Security/compliance deep-dive (FedRAMP, IL-5, CMMC)

**To VP Sales**:
- Deal size >$5M (executive selling required)
- Tier 1 IC agency (CIA, NSA, DIA)
- Palantir sends VP-level exec to meeting

**To Legal**:
- Contract redlines or non-standard terms
- NDA, BAA, or DPA requests
- Intellectual property or licensing questions
