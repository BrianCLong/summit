## Multi-Touch Sequence Framework

### General Best Practices

**Timing**:
- Touch 1: Day 1 (email)
- Touch 2: Day 3-4 (LinkedIn or call)
- Touch 3: Day 7 (email with value-add)
- Touch 4: Day 10 (call)
- Touch 5: Day 15 (break-up email)

**Channel Mix**:
- 60% email
- 20% phone
- 10% LinkedIn
- 10% other (video messages, direct mail, conference)

**Content Ratio**:
- 40% personalization (company-specific insights)
- 30% social proof (case studies, customer quotes)
- 20% value-add (tools, resources, training)
- 10% direct ask (call to action)

**Response Handling**:

| Response Type | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| **Interested (positive reply)** | Book meeting within 48 hours, send calendar invite immediately |
| **Not interested** | Ask permission to follow up in 3-6 months, add to nurture campaign |
| **Wrong person** | Ask for referral, connect with right contact within 24 hours |
| **Bad timing** | Ask when to follow up, set calendar reminder |
| **Out of office** | Pause sequence, resume when they're back |
| **Bounce / Invalid email** | Update CRM, find alternative contact (LinkedIn, company website) |

## Performance Benchmarks

### Expected Metrics by Segment

| Segment | Open Rate | Reply Rate | Meeting Booked | Sequence → Opp |
|---------|-----------|------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Gov/Defense/Intel** | 35-45% | 8-12% | 3-5% | 20-30% |
| **Primes/SIs** | 40-50% | 10-15% | 5-8% | 40-50% |
| **Regulated Enterprise** | 30-40% | 6-10% | 2-4% | 30-40% |
| **NGOs/Think Tanks** | 45-55% | 12-18% | 6-10% | 60-70% |

**Benchmark Notes**:
- **Open Rate**: % of emails opened (industry avg: 20-30%, ours higher due to tight targeting)
- **Reply Rate**: % of emails with any response (positive, negative, or neutral)
- **Meeting Booked**: % of sequences that result in booked discovery call
- **Sequence → Opp**: % of booked meetings that convert to qualified opportunity

### A/B Testing Framework

**Test Variables**:
1. **Subject Lines**:
   - Question vs. statement
   - Personalization (company name) vs. generic
   - Length (short <40 chars vs. long >60 chars)

2. **Email Length**:
   - Short (< 100 words) vs. long (> 200 words)
   - Bullets vs. paragraphs

3. **Call to Action**:
   - Calendar link vs. "reply to this email"
   - Specific time ("15-minute call") vs. vague ("quick chat")

4. **Social Proof**:
   - Customer logos vs. quotes vs. metrics
   - Specific customer names vs. generic "Fortune 500 bank"

**Sample A/B Test**:

| Variant A | Variant B | Winner |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Subject: "Alternative to Palantir?" | Subject: "How DIA Cut Deployment Time by 80%" | **B** (+15% open rate) |
| Email: 75 words, no bullets | Email: 150 words, 3 bullets | **A** (+8% reply rate) |
| CTA: "Grab time here [link]" | CTA: "Reply with your availability" | **A** (+12% meeting booked) |
