# 90-Day Lighthouse Capture Plan: Tier-1 Global Bank Modernization

## Executive Summary

**Objective:** Secure a multi-year, hybrid-deployed intelligence governance contract with a Tier-1 Global Bank within 90 days.
**Target Profile:** Tier-1 bank with a strong modernization agenda, existing Recorded Future (RF) footprint, and board-level pressure around cyber explainability and operational risk.
**Core Wedge:** Do not attack RF head-on. Position Summit CompanyOS as the **Governance Layer** that makes their existing intelligence regulator-ready, defensible, and actionable at the GRC level.

## Phase 1: Power Mapping & Pre-Emptive Posturing (Days 1-30)

### 1. Identify the 'Pain Champions'

- **Target Roles:** Global CISO, Head of Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI), Chief Risk Officer (CRO), Head of IT Audit.
- **The Hook:** Connect cyber telemetry directly to regulatory exposure and litigation defensibility.
- **Actionable Metric:** Map the bank's recent regulatory fines or OCC/SEC warnings regarding cyber governance.

### 2. Narrative Control & The Overlay Pitch

- **The Message:** "Your CTI team is drowning in indicators from RF. We don't replace the feed; we provide the 'Proof Moat'—the cryptographic chain-of-custody that proves to your board and regulators exactly *why* a decision was made."
- **Key Differentiator to Highlight:** Deterministic report hashes, signed bundles, and replayable intelligence pipelines.

### 3. Intelligence Sourcing & Initial Targeting

- **Action Item:** Conduct a deep-dive analysis of the target bank's public-facing attack surface, focusing on financial brand impersonation and payment infrastructure abuse (leveraging FS-ISAC patterns).
- **Deliverable:** A customized, unsolicited mini-brief demonstrating Summit's ability to contextualize a threat they care about, complete with a deterministic evidence hash.

## Phase 2: The Proof Moat Demo & Technical Validation (Days 31-60)

### 1. The CISO Room Demo

- **Focus:** Explainability and Reproducibility.
- **Scenario:** A simulated high-impact ransomware or data extortion threat targeting their payment rails.
- **Execution:** Demonstrate Summit's versioned scoring policies. Show how a generic "Risk 82/100" from a competitor is transformed into a "Regulatory Exposure Modifier" and "Revenue-impact Asset Mapping" within Summit.

### 2. Hybrid Deployment & Sovereignty Deep Dive

- **The Objection Handler:** Banks fear SaaS black boxes.
- **The Solution:** Present the Summit Hybrid/Sovereign architecture. Emphasize the offline-first profile, private VPC capabilities, and strict data residency guarantees.
- **Deliverable:** An architecture document proving that zero sensitive telemetry leaves their sovereign boundaries.

### 3. GRC & Workflow Integration Proof of Concept

- **Integration Targets:** ServiceNow IR, Archer (risk management), and Splunk.
- **Action Item:** Show how a Summit-contextualized threat automatically populates a Jira/ServiceNow ticket with the full cryptographic chain-of-custody attached, satisfying audit requirements instantly.

## Phase 3: Commercial Structuring & Pre-Emptive Lawfare Posture (Days 61-90)

### 1. Constructing the Deal

- **Structure:** Propose an initial 'Overlay License' covering a specific, highly regulated business unit (e.g., Global Trading or Wealth Management) where SaaS is restricted.
- **Pricing Strategy:** Value-based pricing tied to the volume of intelligence processed and governed, not per-seat.

### 2. Pre-Emptive Lawfare & Regulatory Alignment

- **Action Item:** Co-author a 'Cyber Defensibility Charter' with the bank's legal and risk teams.
- **The Value:** Position Summit not just as a tool, but as a compliance mechanism that reduces their liability and insurance premiums. Ensure all deliverables maintain an institutional, controlled, and regulator-aware tone.

### 3. Closing the Lighthouse Logo

- **The Final Push:** Leverage the successful technical validation and the strong alignment with their CRO/Audit teams to force a decision.
- **Success Criteria:** Signed contract, deployed hybrid node in their VPC, and a joint case study (anonymized if necessary) focusing on "Regulator-Ready Intelligence."
