## 2) Written Memo Version (Board Narrative)

### Executive Thesis

Summit is operating in a category transition: enterprise buyers now demand AI systems that can explain and defend outputs under audit pressure, while security and risk teams face a new class of narrative-based threats. Our opportunity is to establish Summit as the infrastructure backbone for evidence-first GraphRAG and narrative intelligence.

### Current Quarter Delivery (Evidence in Repository)

- Governance and evidence contract work is active and directly tied to production gating.
- Narrative operations and cognitive security runbooks now exist to support governed field operations.
- Roadmap and control-plane alignment work indicates a strong platform-level execution cadence.

### Why This Matters Now

- Retrieval quality without explainability is no longer enough in regulated/high-consequence workflows.
- Narrative risk is becoming a budgeted, board-reviewed domain.
- Summit can differentiate by making provenance and governance first-class, not bolt-ons.

### Next Quarter Bets

1. Flagship gray-zone domain playbook.
2. Opinionated GraphRAG starter stack for enterprise teams.
3. Evaluation harness for evidence quality and operator outcomes.
4. Lighthouse partner motion with board-facing proof.
5. Governance-by-default hardening from dev to deployment.

### Core Risks

- ROI proof gap in a noisy AI market.
- Graph extraction cost and operational complexity.
- Category education friction in enterprise procurement.
- Security/misuse risk for narrative mapping capabilities.
- Execution sprawl across too many surfaces.

### KPI Set (Evidence-Backed)

- Time-to-insight reduction.
- Evidence density per answer.
- Provenance completeness.
- Narrative detection lead time.
- Analyst productivity uplift.
- Graph build cost efficiency.
- Governance incident rate.
- Adoption depth.
- Decision impact rate.
- Customer trust signal.

### Kill / Re-Scope Gates

- No KPI uplift in pilot window.
- Persistent provenance gaps.
- Cost-quality envelope failure.
- No lighthouse partner pull.
- Repeated governance exceptions.

### Board-Proof Milestones by End of Next Quarter

- One benchmarked workflow materially outperforming strong baseline.
- One design partner prepared for reference/co-marketing.
- One governed deployment template demonstrating auditability and policy control compatibility.

## 3) KPI Instrumentation Map (How to Plug in Real Summit Data)

| KPI | Primary evidence source | Query or artifact suggestion | Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Time-to-insight | Investigation workflow telemetry + case timestamps | median(case_closed_at - case_opened_at) vs baseline cohort | >=40% reduction |
| Evidence density | Retrieval/evidence payload logs | avg(unique_edges + unique_sources) per high-confidence answer | Up/right trend |
| Provenance completeness | Answer schema validation results | % responses where all key claims map to graph path + citation | >=90% |
| Narrative lead time | Stream ingest timestamps + narrative cluster detection logs | median(cluster_detected_at - first_signal_at) | Down/right trend |
| Productivity uplift | Analyst throughput reports | investigations_per_analyst_week pre/post | +30-50% |
| Graph cost efficiency | LLM usage + infra billing + quality scorecards | cost_per_million_events and quality_uplift ratio | <=2x cost for >=3x quality |
| Governance incident rate | Policy/drift detection logs | escaped incidents vs auto-remediated incidents | Escapes toward 0 |
| Adoption depth | Weekly active users/projects/pipelines | active_teams and active_workflows per week | Up/right trend |
| Decision impact rate | Decision records / PIR templates | % high-severity decisions citing Summit evidence | Up/right trend |
| Customer trust signal | User surveys by persona | explainability NPS / confidence score | Up/right trend |
