# Regulator Interaction Script Library

**Purpose:** To standardize how we speak to regulators. We educate, we demonstrate, we do not "lobby."

---

## Scenario A: The "How Do We Regulate This?" Inquiry

**Regulator:** "This new technology is dangerous. We need strict rules."

**Bad Response (Lobbyist):** "Regulation stifles innovation. Let the market decide. Trust us."

**Clean-Hands Response:**
"We agree that safety is paramount. In fact, we believe the current standards are _too low_.
Here is our internal Safety Model. We have open-sourced the testing harness.
If you are drafting rules, we recommend focusing on **measurable outcomes** (like X and Y) rather than prescribing specific technologies, which will date quickly."

## Scenario B: The Competitor Complaint

**Regulator:** "Competitor X says your approach violates the spirit of the 'Fairness' rule."

**Bad Response (Defensive):** "Competitor X is just jealous/lying. Our lawyers say we are fine."

**Clean-Hands Response:**
"We understand their concern. However, our approach is designed for **auditability**.
Here is a login to our Compliance Portal. You can see the deterministic logic for that decision right now.
Competitor X's system is a black box; ours is an open book. We invite you to compare the traceability of both."

## Scenario C: The "Draft Review" Opportunity

**Regulator:** "We are circulating a draft rule. Do you have comments?"

**Bad Response (Capture):** "Please change Section 4 to exempt us, and we'll support the bill."

**Clean-Hands Response:**
"We have reviewed the draft against our architecture.
Section 4, as written, inadvertently mandates a batch-processing model (legacy).
We suggest phrasing it as 'Real-time or Near-real-time verification,' which allows for modern architectures (like ours) without banning others.
Here is a technical note on why this benefits the consumer."

## Scenario D: The "Help Us Understand" Request

**Regulator:** "We don't understand how [AI/Crypto/Tech] works."

**Bad Response (Condescending/Obscuring):** "It's very complex math. Don't worry about it."

**Clean-Hands Response:**
"We would be happy to host a technical workshop.
We will bring our engineers, not our lawyers.
We will walk you through the system architecture on a whiteboard. No policy requests, just education."

---

**Golden Rule:**
**Always be the most useful person in the room, never the most demanding.**
