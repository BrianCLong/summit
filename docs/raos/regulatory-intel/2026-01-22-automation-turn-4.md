## AI Governance and Regulation

### EU AI Act — Operational Shifts

- 2026 is the readiness year. Core requirements for risk management, transparency, human oversight,
  cybersecurity, and documentation are broadly applicable by **August 2, 2026**, with additional
  high-risk obligations phased through 2027.
  Source: https://www.reedsmith.com/our-insights/blogs/viewpoints/102lyiv/2026-update-eu-regulations-for-tech-and-online-businesses/
- Organizational self-assessment is now central. Providers must self-classify risk categories and
  document evidence of compliance rather than relying on national authority classification.
  Source: https://www.corporatecomplianceinsights.com/eu-ai-act-change-no-one-talking-about/
- The European Artificial Intelligence Office continues to build governance and enforcement capacity
  for general-purpose AI providers, coordinating conformity assessment and surveillance at the EU
  level.
  Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Artificial_Intelligence_Office

**Operational directive:** Accelerate risk classification, self-assessment, and compliance
documentation for EU-bound systems.

## NIST AI RMF and U.S. Policy Signals

### NIST AI RMF Adoption Trends

- The NIST AI RMF remains the U.S. baseline for AI risk governance. Embed Govern, Map, Measure,
  and Manage into enterprise risk management workflows.
  Source: https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework
- The preliminary draft Cyber AI Profile is attracting attention as a reference for integrating
  AI-specific cybersecurity risk into security operations.
  Source: https://natlawreview.com/article/nist-issues-preliminary-draft-cyber-ai-profile-framework-poised-alter-security

**Operational directive:** Integrate AI RMF governance and measurement controls across security and
compliance pipelines.
