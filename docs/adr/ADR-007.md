# ADR-007: Evidence Confidence Scoring Model

## Status
Accepted

## Context
Intelligence products delivered by Summit must be transparent about their reliability. Analysts need to know the confidence level of the information retrieved and synthesized by the GraphRAG pipeline. A single, opaque "relevance score" is insufficient for professional intelligence analysis. We need a multi-factor scoring model that considers retrieval relevance, graph structural importance, and the intrinsic reliability of the source data.

## Decision
We have adopted a **Multi-Factor Confidence Scoring Model** for all evidence chunks and synthesized answers.

The composite confidence score is calculated based on:
1. **Retrieval Relevance (Vector)**: The cosine similarity of the query embedding to the evidence chunk.
2. **Structural Saliency (Graph)**: A score derived from graph algorithms (PageRank, Betweenness Centrality) indicating the importance of the entities involved within the local and global sub-graph.
3. **Source Reliability**: A weight assigned to the source of the data (e.g., human intelligence vs. unverified OSINT).
4. **Lineage Stability**: A factor based on the consistency of the evidence over time and across multiple ingestion sources.

All scores are normalized to a 0.0–1.0 range and are presented to the analyst alongside the synthesized reasoning.

## Consequences
- **Positive**: Provides high transparency and auditability for intelligence products. Allows analysts to filter evidence by confidence levels and focus on high-reliability information.
- **Negative**: Increases the complexity of the retrieval and synthesis pipeline. Requires a well-maintained source reliability registry.
- **Neutral**: The scoring model must be periodically tuned and validated against human expert judgments to ensure its accuracy.
