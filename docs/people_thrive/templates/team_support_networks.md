# Team Support Networks & Community Playbook

## Purpose

To provide safe outlets for connection, reflection, and recharge across the Summit organization.

## Types of Networks

### 1. Staff Communities (Interest-based)

- **Goal**: Connect people with shared interests or backgrounds.
- **Example**: Women in Engineering, DevOps Enthusiasts, Parents at Summit.

### 2. Peer Support Groups (Role-based)

- **Goal**: Shared reflection and problem-solving for specific roles.
- **Example**: Manager Guild, Frontend Roundtables.

### 3. Safe Outlets (Process-based)

- **Goal**: Confidential spaces for feedback and support.
- **Example**: Office Hours, Anonymized feedback channels.

## How to Start a Network

1. **Identify the Need**: Is there a gap in support or connection?
2. **Find a Sponsor**: A leader to provide visibility and resources.
3. **Define the Cadence**: Monthly syncs, async Slack channel, or quarterly workshops.
4. **Establish Ground Rules**: Ensure psychological safety (Chatham House Rules).

## Measuring Impact

- Participation rates (voluntary).
- Feedback from members on the value of the network.
- Integration with the People Thrive metrics.
