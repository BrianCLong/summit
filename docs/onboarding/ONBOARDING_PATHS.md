# Customer Onboarding Paths

This document provides role-specific onboarding paths for Summit customers. Each path is designed to help users understand their capabilities, limitations, and responsibilities within the platform.

---

## 1. Admin / Security Role

**Objective:** Configure the platform, manage users and security settings, and ensure compliance.

### First-Day Checklist

1.  **[ ] Log in and review your Admin dashboard.**
2.  **[ ] Integrate with your organization's Single Sign-On (SSO) provider.**
3.  **[ ] Review and configure default security policies and access controls.**
4.  **[ ] Invite your first team of users and assign them to their appropriate roles.**
5.  **[ ] Familiarize yourself with the audit log and reporting features.**

### What You Can Do

- Manage users, groups, and roles.
- Configure platform-wide security settings, including authentication and authorization policies.
- Define and manage data access policies and controls.
- Monitor system activity and audit logs for security and compliance purposes.
- Integrate Summit with other security tools and systems.

### What You Cannot Do

- Perform data analysis or investigations (this is typically reserved for Analyst roles).
- Bypass audit logging or security controls.

### Required Approvals and Limits

- Certain high-risk operations, such as deleting critical data or disabling security features, may require a two-person approval process.
- Rate limits are in place for API access and other automated interactions to prevent abuse.

---

## 2. Operator Role

**Objective:** Manage the day-to-day operations of the platform, including data ingestion, system health, and basic troubleshooting.

### First-Day Checklist

1.  **[ ] Log in and review the Operator dashboard.**
2.  **[ ] Check the status of data ingestion pipelines and connectors.**
3.  **[ ] Review system health metrics and alerts.**
4.  **[ ] Familiarize yourself with the process for troubleshooting common issues.**
5.  **[ ] Understand the process for escalating issues to the Admin/Security team or support.**

### What You Can Do

- Monitor and manage data ingestion pipelines.
- Perform routine system maintenance tasks.
- Troubleshoot and resolve common operational issues.
- Manage system resources and capacity.

### What You Cannot Do

- Change security policies or user access controls.
- Perform in-depth data analysis or investigations.

### Required Approvals and Limits

- Actions that may impact system stability or data integrity, such as restarting critical services, may require approval from an Admin.
- Limits are in place for concurrent data ingestion processes to ensure system performance.

---

## 3. Analyst / Power User Role

**Objective:** Use the platform to perform data analysis, conduct investigations, and generate insights.

### First-Day Checklist

1.  **[ ] Log in and explore your personalized workspace.**
2.  **[ ] Run a predefined search or query to familiarize yourself with the data.**
3.  **[ ] Create your first investigation and add data to it.**
4.  **[ ] Learn how to use the platform's visualization and analysis tools.**
5.  **[ ] Understand the process for requesting access to additional data or features.**

### What You Can Do

- Search, query, and analyze data.
- Create and manage investigations.
- Use visualization and analysis tools to explore data and identify patterns.
- Collaborate with other analysts on investigations.
- Export data and generate reports.

### What You Cannot Do

- Modify data sources or ingestion pipelines.
- Change system-wide settings or security policies.

### Required Approvals and Limits

- Access to sensitive data sets may require approval from an Admin or data owner.
- Limits are in place on the amount of data that can be exported at one time.
- Complex or long-running queries may be subject to resource limits to ensure fair usage.

---

## 4. Executive / Viewer Role

**Objective:** View high-level dashboards and reports to monitor key metrics and stay informed about the business.

### First-Day Checklist

1.  **[ ] Log in and view your primary dashboard.**
2.  **[ ] Learn how to navigate between different dashboards and reports.**
3.  **[ ] Understand how to use filters and other controls to customize your view.**
4.  **[ ] Subscribe to receive regular email summaries of key reports.**

### What You Can Do

- View and interact with predefined dashboards and reports.
- Filter and drill down into data to explore high-level trends.
- Export dashboards and reports in various formats (e.g., PDF, CSV).

### What You Cannot Do

- Perform ad-hoc queries or create new visualizations.
- Modify dashboards or reports.
- Access raw data or perform investigations.

### Required Approvals and Limits

- This role is read-only and does not require any special approvals for its core functions.
- Access to specific dashboards may be restricted based on the user's business unit or function.
