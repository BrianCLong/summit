# Summit Segment Prioritization Brief (2026-03-11)

## Readiness Assertion

This brief aligns with Summit's readiness posture: move first on segments where multi-agent orchestration and governance evidence are already budgeted buying criteria, then scale playbooks horizontally.

## Decision (Single Segment to Target First)

**Target first: Compliance / Risk / Surveillance leaders in regulated industries (especially financial services).**

## Why this segment wins now

1. **Immediate buying urgency:** Organizations are already shifting to AI-powered detection and summarization for supervisory review and need deployment-ready systems this budget cycle.
2. **Governance pressure is explicit:** ISO/IEC 42001 and EU AI Act timelines convert governance from future concern into current procurement requirement.
3. **Summit differentiation is strongest here:** Graph-native decision traces, control mapping, and exportable evidence bundles are highest-value in regulated review workflows.
4. **Competitive insulation:** Vendor-native "agent teams" from model providers are less complete on cross-system evidence lineage and policy-governed audit output.
5. **Fastest proof loop:** Compliance teams can validate value quickly via reduced triage time, better alert explainability, and audit-ready evidence completeness.

## ICP definition for first wave

- **Functions:** Head of Compliance, Head of Risk, Surveillance Director, Model Risk Governance lead.
- **Industries:** Financial services first; then insurance and regulated fintech.
- **Buying trigger:** Backlog of alerts/cases, regulator pressure for explainability, need to operationalize AI controls.
- **Success metrics:**
  - reduced time from alert to disposition,
  - increased evidence completeness per case,
  - lower manual summarization burden,
  - measurable audit preparation time reduction.

## 30-day execution plan

1. Publish a compliance-focused positioning page mapping Summit capabilities to ISO/IEC 42001 control themes and EU AI Act high-risk documentation expectations.
2. Ship a demo flow: detection -> graph-linked investigation -> agent decision trace -> evidence bundle export.
3. Launch outbound sequence targeting compliance/risk leaders with quantitative hook on AI detection/summarization adoption.
4. Add pricing narrative that emphasizes governed orchestration capacity and cost transparency, not per-agent framing.
5. Standardize one proof-of-value template with baseline and target KPI deltas for 2-week pilot cycles.

## Messaging spine (external)

Summit is the graph-native orchestration and governance fabric for regulated AI operations, enabling multi-agent workflows with traceable decisions, control-linked evidence, and audit-ready outputs across vendor boundaries.
