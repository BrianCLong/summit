# Product Narrative: The Proof Moat

## Introduction
The "Proof Moat" is Summit's strategic differentiator against legacy threat intelligence platforms. It centers on delivering **Actionability + Provability**. For Tier-1 Financial Services (FS) and Regulated Investigations/Legal teams, discovery alone is insufficient. They require defensible, mathematically verified actions.

## Strategic Positioning
*   **Defensibility over Discovery:** We augment global threat feeds (e.g., Recorded Future) as a governance and orchestration layer.
*   **Governed Automation:** We avoid unstructured "vibe coding" in favor of Agentic Engineering. Every autonomous action is strictly bound by deterministic rules, cryptographically signed evidence bundles, and Prompt Injection/Tool Boundary Gates.

## The 4 Core Capabilities (Minimal Proof Moat MVP)
These are the foundational capabilities of Summit designed for Enterprise Trust and regulatory compliance.

### 1. Deterministic Replay
Every action produced by our agents is bound to a W3C PROV-compliant lineage. Using deterministic hashes (e.g., SHA-256) and stable sorting (avoiding wall-clock timestamps or random UUIDs), we ensure that the exact context, inputs, toolchains, and transforms leading to an AI decision can be perfectly replayed and audited.

### 2. Explainable Scoring
Summit’s scoring and risk assessments are fully explainable. We provide clear reasoning traces (Reasoning-Trace Hasher/Signatures) tied to stable sources in an Evidence Map. This eliminates black-box AI logic, giving auditors clear, causal links for every decision.

### 3. Policy Versioning
Governance and readiness policies are strictly versioned. Policy changes immediately affect the execution boundary without corrupting historical lineage. The Evidence Ledger tracks which version of a policy was active when an action occurred, ensuring robust compliance over time.

### 4. Hybrid-Ready Architecture
Designed for the most stringent data residency requirements, Summit supports a hybrid-ready architecture out of the box. Leveraging Proof-Carrying Queries (PCQ) and the Zero-Knowledge Trust Exchange (ZK-TX), enterprise clients can keep sensitive data localized while still orchestrating global intelligence effectively.
