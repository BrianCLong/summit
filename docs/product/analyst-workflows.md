# Summit Analyst Workflows: "Day in the Life"

This document outlines three concrete workflows for analysts interacting with Summit’s multi-agent stack, highlighting the integration between frontend UX (Cognitive Weather Radar, Decision Strip, 'Why?' panel) and backend governance (Maestro engine state transitions, Epistemic Assurance Plane, and IntelGraph).

## Workflow 1: Triage & The Decision Strip (Routine Alert Handling)

**Scenario:** A Tier 1 analyst logs into the single pane of glass and needs to triage a medium-confidence alert regarding a newly registered typosquatting domain.

1. **The Analyst Inbox:** The analyst opens the Inbox queue and selects the top-priority alert.
2. **The Investigation Canvas:** The canvas loads, displaying a summary card generated by Maestro.
3. **The 'Why?' Panel:** The analyst clicks "Explain This." The 'Why?' panel slides out, revealing the chain of custody. It displays the DNS registration evidence artifact (`evidence.json` from the connector SDK) and shows Maestro's confidence score (e.g., 65%). The panel provides dual views:
    * *Analyst View:* A natural language summary ("Domain registered 2 hours ago, similar to our primary brand, no MX records yet").
    * *Governance View:* Cypher query links to the specific nodes in IntelGraph, showing the bitemporal ledger entries and OpenLineage metadata.
4. **The Decision Strip:** Because the confidence is below the auto-quarantine threshold (set at 80%), Maestro has placed the alert in a "Candidate" state (referencing the `summit-lane-model`). The Decision Strip prompts the analyst for action:
    * *State:* Candidate
    * *Action Required:* Review & Classify
5. **Analyst Action:** The analyst reviews the 'Why?' panel, agrees with the assessment, and clicks **Approve & Promote** in the Decision Strip.
6. **Backend Transition:** The `summit-maestro-events` engine registers a `claim.register` event. The domain's state in IntelGraph transitions from `candidate` to `observed`. An epistemic event records the analyst's approval, feeding back into Maestro's calibration loop.

## Workflow 2: Cognitive Weather Radar & IntelGraph (Deep Threat Hunting)

**Scenario:** A Threat Hunter is investigating a complex disinformation campaign targeting a specific geopolitical region.

1. **Cognitive Weather Radar:** The hunter opens the CogGeo dashboard. The map (rendered via `mapbox-gl` and vector tiles from `summit-coggeo`) displays a "Narrative Storm" heat map over the target region, indicating a sudden spike in specific claims.
2. **IntelGraph Drill-Down:** The hunter clicks on a high-intensity H3 cell. The view transitions to an IntelGraph visualization of the entities within that cell.
3. **Visual Exploration:** The graph shows a network of newly created accounts (amplifiers) pushing a central `NARRATIVE` node.
4. **Maestro Interaction:** The hunter selects the `NARRATIVE` node and uses the **Explain This** feature. Maestro generates a summary of the narrative's propagation, linking to the supporting `EVIDENCE` nodes (raw posts, timestamps).
5. **The Audit Rail:** The hunter notices a specific account that seems central to the network. They use an OSINT tool via the Evidence Board to enrich the account profile. The Inline Audit Rail records this action: "Analyst [Name] ran [Tool] on [Entity] at [Time]."
6. **Outcome:** The hunter identifies the campaign's origin. They package the findings into an Evidence Bundle, exporting the deterministic graph state, policy tests, and the Audit Rail for reporting.

## Workflow 3: Overriding an Agent & Quarantine (Handling Exceptions)

**Scenario:** Maestro automatically flags an internal employee's IP address as "Malicious" due to an unusual login pattern and places the related node in the "Quarantined" lane.

1. **Alert Notification:** A senior analyst receives a high-priority notification about the quarantined internal IP.
2. **Investigation Canvas:** They open the alert. The Decision Strip shows:
    * *State:* Quarantined (Auto-enforced by Maestro)
    * *Confidence:* 92%
3. **The 'Why?' Panel:** The analyst reviews the 'Why?' panel. Maestro's rationale is based on a rigid behavioral baseline rule.
4. **Contextual Override:** The analyst knows this employee is running authorized penetration testing tools from that IP (a context Maestro lacked).
5. **Inline Audit Rail Controls:** The analyst uses the governance controls in the Audit Rail to initiate an override. They click **Override Agent**.
6. **Justification:** A modal requires the analyst to provide a freeform rationale ("Authorized pentest in progress, see ticket #1234"). Crucially, the Epistemic Assurance Plane enforces the "never-log" policy—if the analyst includes sensitive tokens in the justification, they are masked or blocked before saving.
7. **Backend Transition:**
    * The `summit-lane-model` transitions the node from `quarantined` back to `trusted`.
    * Maestro captures this override (downgrade when approved by the agent) and feeds it into the epistemic calibration loop.
    * The `RiskGNNService` logs the anomaly and the human intervention for future model tuning.
