# Product Requirements Document: Summit Analyst Assist v0.2
## Governed OSINT Lead Desk

### 1. Executive Summary

**Vision:** Move the control-plane OSINT loop from a "lab setup" demo into a repeatable, opinionated product surface. Analyst Assist v0.2 transforms ad-hoc intelligence gathering into a governed, reproducible workflow designed for Tier-1 Financial Services and SOCs.

**Goal:** Create a minimal but resilient UX ("Governed OSINT Lead Desk") with a stable entry point, introduce a critical analyst feedback loop to train the Maestro agent, and establish baseline metrics for "time-to-first-governed-lead".

**Target Audience:** SOC Analysts, Threat Intelligence Analysts, and Compliance/GRC Officers in highly regulated environments.

### 2. Core Value Proposition

"We help intelligence analysts generate actionable, policy-compliant leads 10x faster by automating the OSINT loop with a deterministic, evidence-backed agent, while keeping the analyst firmly in the decision seat."

### 3. Key Features & Scope (MVP v0.2)

#### 3.1 Stable Entry Point & Minimal UX
*   **Named Feature:** "Analyst Assist" accessible via a stable URL (e.g., `/analyst-assist`).
*   **Tri-Pane Layout:**
    *   **Left Pane (Lead List):** A queue of OSINT leads generated by the Maestro agent, showing status (New, Approved, Downgraded, Blocked), risk score, and basic metadata.
    *   **Center Pane (Detail/Graph):** Visualization of the specific lead, showing the extracted artifact graph, lineage, and explainable scoring breakdown.
    *   **Right Pane (Decision & Evidence):** The analyst workspace containing the policy overlay applied, a link to the immutable evidence bundle (`SUM-EV1::...`), and action buttons (Approve, Downgrade, Block).

#### 3.2 The Epistemic Feedback Loop
*   **Capture Analyst Overrides:** When an analyst disagrees with the agent's assessment (e.g., downgrades an "Approved" lead, or approves a "Blocked" lead due to context).
*   **Write to IntelGraph:** These override events must be written back into the IntelGraph as *epistemic events* (claims about claims) associated with the user.
*   **Agent Calibration:** This data will be used by Maestro in future iterations to learn calibration and adjust its scoring/policy application based on human-in-the-loop corrections.

#### 3.3 The Evidence Ledger Integration
*   Ensure every lead displayed in the UI has a clear, one-click path to its deterministic Evidence Bundle and Bundle Hash.
*   "Show the Math": The UI must explicitly show the policy version and rule that generated the alert/lead.

### 4. Success Metrics (v0.3 Goals defined in v0.2)

To prove this is a repeatable product surface, we will measure:

1.  **Time to First Governed Lead:** < 15 minutes from a fresh repository clone to viewing a populated lead in the Analyst Assist UI.
2.  **Analyst Actions per Session:** Tracking the number of Approve/Downgrade/Block actions taken to measure engagement and loop efficiency.
3.  **Evidence Attachment Rate:** % of published/approved leads that have a fully valid, cryptographic evidence bundle attached before being sent downstream (e.g., to Splunk/Sentinel). Target: 100%.

### 5. Out of Scope (For Now)
*   Massive new OSINT integrations (stick to the existing SpiderFoot/mocked connectors).
*   Advanced ML scoring models (stick to explainable, rule-based policies).
*   Cross-campaign graph analysis.
*   Full SIEM integration (pushing to Splunk is a later step, MVP just proves the workflow).

### 6. User Journey Map

1.  **Discovery:** Analyst logs in and navigates to the "Analyst Assist" dashboard.
2.  **Triage:** Analyst selects a "New" lead from the queue.
3.  **Investigation:** Analyst reviews the graph, score breakdown, and policy rules applied in the center pane.
4.  **Verification:** Analyst clicks to view the underlying Evidence Ledger to confirm determinism.
5.  **Action:** Analyst decides the agent over-scored the risk and clicks "Downgrade".
6.  **Feedback Loop:** The system records this downgrade as an epistemic event in IntelGraph, updating the lead status and signaling Maestro.
