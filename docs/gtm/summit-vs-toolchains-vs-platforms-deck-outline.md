# Deck Outline — Summit vs Toolchains vs Platforms

Version: 2026-02-11
Audience: CRO, Sales Engineering, Strategic Accounts
Length: 16–20 slides

## Narrative Arc

1. **Market problem:** Investigations are fragmented across tools, exports, and analyst-dependent workflows.
2. **Procurement confusion:** Buyers conflate point tools, data feeds, and investigation platforms.
3. **Decision frame:** Evaluate by reproducibility, governance, and time-to-outcome.
4. **Summit thesis:** Governed investigation runtime above toolchains; replacement where runtime is missing.

## Slide-by-Slide Structure

### Slide 1 — Title

- Summit vs Toolchains vs Platforms
- Subtitle: Evidence-first investigations at operational speed

### Slide 2 — Executive Summary

- Toolchains optimize tasks.
- Platforms optimize operations.
- Summit optimizes **defensible outcomes**.

### Slide 3 — Category Confusion Map

- X-axis: workflow maturity (ad hoc → operationalized)
- Y-axis: governance maturity (implicit → policy-enforced)
- Place vendors by profile (tool, feed, platform)

### Slide 4 — Buyer Decision Rubric

- Reproducibility
- Policy control
- Agentic execution
- Cross-domain fusion
- Time-to-value

### Slide 5 — Summit Architecture Snapshot

- Evidence graph
- Playbooks-as-code
- Policy gates
- Replay/diff engine
- Integration plane

### Slide 6 — Kill-Shot Questions

- Present the five disqualifying questions used in discovery

### Slide 7 — ShadowDragon / Social Links / Maltego (Toolchain Cluster)

- What they do well
- Where they fail in governed runtime depth
- Summit posture: complement → consolidate over time

### Slide 8 — IBM i2 / Blackdot / Skopenow (Investigation Suite Cluster)

- Incumbent strengths
- Summit differentiation by reproducibility + playbook lifecycle

### Slide 9 — Cyabra / OSINT Industries (Specialist Signals Cluster)

- Strong specialist signal generation
- Summit as synthesis + case operations layer

### Slide 10 — Recorded Future / Orpheus (Intelligence & Risk Cluster)

- Feed and rating strengths
- Summit runtime for mixed-domain decisions

### Slide 11 — Graphistry (Visualization Cluster)

- Visualization as interface, not operating system
- Summit as governed data/runtime plane

### Slide 12 — Palantir (Enterprise Platform Cluster)

- Enterprise breadth and governance footprint
- Summit speed, specialization, and lower deployment drag

### Slide 13 — Complement vs Replace Framework

- Decision tree:
  - Keep as feed
  - Keep as analyst edge tool
  - Replace where no governed runtime exists

### Slide 14 — Competitive Proof Demo Script

- Run playbook
- Export evidence bundle
- Replay + diff
- Show policy/audit logs

### Slide 15 — ROI and Risk Compression

- Analyst hours saved
- Mean time to defensible report
- Audit prep reduction
- Integration surface reduction

### Slide 16 — Migration/Land Strategy

- 30-day pilot
- 90-day expansion
- 2–3 incumbent integrations retained initially

### Slide 17 — Objection Handling Appendix

- “We already have X.”
- “We need one platform.”
- “We can build this.”

### Slide 18 — Close

- Summit is the governed investigation runtime.
- Toolchain where useful, platform where necessary, defensibility by default.

## Speaker Notes Checklist

- Always cite concrete evidence over claims.
- Use complement-first language with entrenched incumbents.
- Convert feature debates into governance + reproducibility tests.
- End with deployment speed and defensibility metrics.

## Demo Readiness Checklist

- Preloaded cross-domain case dataset.
- One approved playbook with version history.
- Evidence bundle export artifact.
- Replay/diff output from a second execution.
- Policy gate logs with user, role, action, and justification.
