# Deck outline (slide-by-slide, with speaker notes)

**Deck title:** *Summit — the Governed Investigation Runtime (Agentic OSINT + Evidence Graph)*
**Target length:** 20–25 slides (+ appendix).

---

## Slide 1 — Title

* Summit: Governed, Agentic Investigations over an Evidence-First Intelligence Graph
**Notes:** One sentence: “We turn ad-hoc investigations into reproducible, auditable playbooks.”

---

## Slide 2 — The problem: investigations don’t scale

* Investigations are still artisanal: toolchains, screenshots, tribal SOPs
* Results vary by analyst; audit defensibility is fragile
* Automation exists, but governance + provenance don’t
**Notes:** Name the pain: “If this went to court/regulator/board—can you replay it?”

---

## Slide 3 — What changed: agents + adversaries + regulation

* Adversaries operate faster, more coordinated, more synthetic
* Teams need *repeatability* and *defensibility*, not more dashboards
**Notes:** Set up why “tool-first OSINT” is insufficient.

---

## Slide 4 — Summit in one line

* **A governed investigation runtime** that executes **versioned playbooks** over an **evidence-first intelligence graph**, producing **audit-ready evidence bundles**
**Notes:** This is the category claim.

---

## Slide 5 — The Summit architecture (conceptual)

* Intelligence graph (entities/relationships/evidence)
* Agentic playbook runtime (tools + workflows + guardrails)
* Governance plane (approvals, policy, drift detection, provenance)
**Notes:** Show how you’re not “yet another UI.”

---

## Slide 6 — The investigation lifecycle you automate

* Ingest → normalize → fuse → analyze → report → monitor
**Notes:** Emphasize consistent outputs + replay.

---

## Slide 7 — Demo scenario (pick one)

Option A: Executive impersonation / deepfake-driven fraud attempt
Option B: Third-party compromise / vendor risk escalation
**Notes:** Make it vivid and enterprise-relevant.

---

## Slide 8 — What the demo shows (must-hit proof points)

* Playbook run
* Evidence capture + citations
* Re-run + diff
* Policy gate (who/what/why)
**Notes:** This is where competitors fail in live demos.

---

## Slide 9 — Governance and auditability (the moat)

* Evidence bundles (hashes, captured artifacts, citations)
* Deterministic outputs + replay
* Change detection + drift control
**Notes:** Anchor on “this survives adversarial scrutiny.”

---

## Slide 10 — Integration posture (how Summit coexists)

* Summit can sit above existing tools (Maltego/i2, RF, etc.)
* Or replace where the buyer wants a unified runtime
**Notes:** Reduce buyer fear; “we don’t force a rip-and-replace.”

---

## Slide 11 — Where Summit fits in the market map

* OSINT workbenches (Maltego/i2)
* OSINT platforms (ShadowDragon, Social Links, Videris, Skopenow)
* CTI platforms (Recorded Future)
* Risk ratings (Orpheus)
* Enterprise fusion (Palantir)
**Notes:** Your “intersection” wedge.

---

## Slide 12 — Competitive thesis: tools vs platforms vs runtimes

* Tools: great for pivots, weak for standardization/governance
* Platforms: great for workflows, weak for reproducibility/playbooks-as-code
* Summit: runtime + evidence graph + governance
**Notes:** This sets up the battlecards.

---

## Slide 13 — Battlecard slide: Maltego / i2 toolchains

* Maltego entry price is low (€149/yr) → fast adoption ([Maltego][3])
* i2 is entrenched enterprise visual analysis (2,000+ orgs claim) ([i2 Group][4])
* Summit value: reproducible playbooks + evidence bundles + policy gates
**Notes:** “Keep your microscopes; adopt our lab.”

---

## Slide 14 — Battlecard slide: OSINT platforms (ShadowDragon / Social Links / Videris / Skopenow)

* ShadowDragon emphasizes OPSEC + SOC 2 Type II ([ShadowDragon][1])
* Social Links Crimewall claims 500+ sources + reporting exports ([Social Links][2])
* Skopenow pitches AI models + situational awareness + link analysis ([Skopenow][6])
* Summit win: playbooks-as-code + replay/diff + governance plane
**Notes:** “They sell features; we sell governed outcomes.”

---

## Slide 15 — Battlecard slide: Recorded Future (CTI)

* RF Intelligence Graph: 1M+ sources, incl. open/dark web + telemetry ([Recorded Future][9])
* Summit: broader investigations + evidence-first casework; RF can remain a feed
**Notes:** Show “RF + Summit” coexistence path.

---

## Slide 16 — Battlecard slide: Orpheus (insurance/TPRM ratings)

* Summit: investigations + evidence; Orpheus is a scoring signal source
**Notes:** This slide sells partnerships/interop.

## Slide 17 — Battlecard slide: Cyabra (narratives/disinfo)

* Narrative Alerts positioned as real-time early warning; Feb 5, 2026 page ([Cyabra][8])
* Summit: turns narrative signals into full investigations (corroboration + evidence graph)
**Notes:** “Signals vs cases.”

* Foundry: integration beyond typical ETL/ELT ([Palantir][18])

**Notes:** “Out-focus + out-ship.”
