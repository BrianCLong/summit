# Financial Services Parity Matrix: Summit vs. Recorded Future

**Owner:** Jules
**Status:** Active
**Target Audience:** Financial Services CISOs, Risk Officers, and Threat Intel Directors

## Overview
This document outlines Summit’s strategic positioning alongside Recorded Future (RF) within the Financial Services (FS) sector.

**Core Narrative:** Summit is not a replacement for global threat feeds. Instead, Summit is the **governance and orchestration layer** that makes intelligence regulator-ready and defensible. Keep RF as your global feed; use Summit to make that intelligence actionable, auditable, and compliant with strict financial regulations (e.g., SEC, OCC, PRA, MAS).

## The Parity Matrix

| Capability Area | Recorded Future (Global Feed) | Summit (Governance OS) | FS Value Proposition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Intelligence Sourcing** | Broad, proprietary collection network across open, deep, and dark web. | Integrates sector-specific feeds (e.g., FS-ISAC), fraud/carding telemetry, and internal alerts. | Leverage existing RF investments while enriching with highly specific financial threat vectors. |
| **Scoring & Prioritization** | Black-box risk scores (e.g., 0-100) based on global trends. | Customizable, version-controlled scoring policies (e.g., "Regulatory Exposure Modifier", "Revenue-impact Asset Mapping"). | Translate generic risk scores into enterprise-specific operational risk metrics understood by the Board. |
| **Explainability & Audit** | Links to raw intelligence sources, but opaque algorithmic weighting. | Deterministic report hashes, verifiable data chains, and EVID-tagged provenance for every decision. | Unassailable defensibility during regulatory audits and litigation discovery. |
| **Deployment & Data Residency** | Predominantly SaaS, subject to standard cloud sovereignty concerns. | Hybrid, air-gapped, and local-first runtime profiles with strict VPC/on-prem controls. | Complete compliance with strict data locality controls and vendor risk review frameworks. |
| **Integration & Workflow** | Strong standard integrations (SIEM/SOAR). | Deep, policy-gated integration into GRC platforms (Archer, ServiceNow IR) and custom orchestrated workflows. | Seamless embedding of intelligence into existing risk management and remediation pipelines. |
| **Reproducibility** | Point-in-time assessments that may shift as backend models update. | Replayable pipelines and signed bundles ensuring historical decisions can be perfectly reconstructed. | Prove *exactly* what was known and why a specific action was taken at any given moment in the past. |

## Execution Guidance
When engaging FS prospects:
1. **Acknowledge the RF investment:** Affirm the value of their broad visibility.
2. **Pivot to the Governance Gap:** Ask how they prove the validity of their prioritization models to regulators.
3. **Introduce the Proof Moat:** Demonstrate how Summit wraps their existing intelligence (including RF) in a deterministic, auditable, and sovereign architecture.
