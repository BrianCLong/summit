# Political Stability Analysis

## Objective
Assess political stability indicators to identify risks of government instability, elite fractures, and institutional weakness.

## Analysis Framework

### Key Indicators
1. **Elite Cohesion**: Analyze relationships among political, military, and economic elites
2. **Government Effectiveness**: Evaluate capacity to deliver public services and maintain order
3. **Institutional Strength**: Assess independence and resilience of key institutions
4. **Political Violence**: Track protests, demonstrations, and violent incidents
5. **Electoral Integrity**: Evaluate fairness and acceptance of electoral processes

### Data Sources (Legitimate Only)
- Public government statistics
- International organization reports (UN, World Bank, IMF)
- Academic research and policy institutes
- Open-source media analysis
- Economic indicators and public opinion polls

### Risk Assessment Levels
- **LOW (0-25)**: Stable institutions, peaceful transfers of power, high elite cohesion
- **MODERATE (26-50)**: Some tensions, but institutions functioning
- **HIGH (51-75)**: Significant instability, institutional stress, elite conflicts
- **CRITICAL (76-100)**: Imminent risk of major political disruption

### Analysis Output
Provide:
1. Current stability score (0-100)
2. Trend analysis (improving/stable/deteriorating)
3. Key risk factors and early warning signs
4. Confidence level in assessment
5. Recommended monitoring focus areas

### Ethical Guidelines
- Use only publicly available information
- Respect sovereignty and self-determination
- Focus on humanitarian early warning, not regime manipulation
- Maintain analytical objectivity and avoid bias
- Clearly state confidence levels and uncertainties

### Use Cases
- Humanitarian planning and intervention
- Business continuity and risk management
- Diplomatic engagement strategy
- Academic research and policy analysis
- Early warning for refugee flows and humanitarian crises
